World Wildlife Fund Project - Earth Hour

2014

During Earth Hour, hundreds of millions of people around the world turn off their lights for one hour to show their commitment to helping the planet.

For Earth Hour 2014 I collaborated with program staff to develop a widget for Earth Hour participants. This module allowed users to signup for Earth Hour and collected their information through a Convio API. This widget was also responsive, so if the user happened to be on a mobile device, it would ask for their phone number and if they would like to receive text notifications.

The module was also visually dynamic. It changed its appearance depending on the number of sign ups and kept a running tally at the top. Every interaction with the module was also tracked through Google Analytics Event Tracking so that its performance could be analyzed. In the days leading up to Earth Hour 2014, this module captured thousands of signups and valuable information for further fund raising efforts.

World Wildlife Fund Project - Solar Energy Quiz

2013

Another project of mine at the WWF was an interactive quiz geared towards promoting WWF's campaign to increase awareness on solar energy. You can still experience it yourself here.

I collaborated with program staff to determine the content of the quiz. I then planned the UX and design of the quiz and coded it using HTML, CSS and JavaScript / JQuery.

The quiz is completely responsive and features animations, different outcomes depending on your score, and was coded so that every click and score was tracked with Google Event Tracking. This enabled WWF to determine the popularity of the quiz, which questions the public answered correct or incorrect, and how many shared the quiz or used its call to action. Upon launch, this quiz was taken by tens of thousands of WWF supporters.
